\section {Exercises on factorization into A = LU}
\paragraph{Problem 4.1:} What matrix $E$ puts $A$ into triangular form $EA = U$? Multi­ply by$ E^{−1} = L$ to factor $A$ into $LU$.

$$
A =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 3 & 0 \\
2 & 4 & 0 \\
2 & 0 & 1 
\end{bmatrix}
$$
$$
E_{21} =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
-2 & 1 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1 
\end{bmatrix}
E_{31} = 
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 1 & 0 \\
-2 & 0 & 1 
\end{bmatrix}
E_{32} = 
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & 1 & 0 \\
0 & -3 & 1 
\end{bmatrix}
$$
$$
A=\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 3 & 0 \\
2 & 4 & 0 \\
2 & 0 & 1 
\end{bmatrix}
=LU=
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
2 & 1 & 0 \\
2 & 3 & 1 
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 3 & 0 \\
0 & -2 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1 
\end{bmatrix}
$$

\paragraph {Problem 4.2:} Compute L and U for the symmetric matrix. Find four conditions on a, b, c, d to get A = LU with four pivots.
$$
A=
\begin{bmatrix}
a & a & a & a \\
a & b & b & b  \\
a & b & c & c \\
a & b & c & d \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$$
U = 
\begin{bmatrix}
a & a & a & a \\
0 & b-a & b-a & b-a  \\
0 & 0 & c-b & c-b \\
0 & 0 & 0 & d-c \\
\end{bmatrix}
L=
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
1 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
1 & 1 & 1 & 0 \\
1 & 1 & 1 & 1 \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$
.The pivot positions cannot be equal to zero. So a can't be zero, b can't equal a, and c can't equal b or d.
